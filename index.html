Why Massage Therapy?

A Career That Truly
Makes a Difference

Massage therapy isn't just a job — it's a profession built around helping people feel better every single day.

Whether you're drawn to healthcare, wellness, sports, or spa environments, this career offers a unique blend of purpose and flexibility that few professions can match.

The wellness industry continues to grow as more people prioritize their physical and mental health. Massage therapists are in demand across clinics, resorts, corporate wellness programs, rehabilitation centers, and private practice.

If you're considering a career change — or exploring your options for the first time — massage therapy offers a clear, achievable path to meaningful work with a schedule you control.

  • Help people recover, relax, and heal
  • Work in diverse settings — spas, clinics, sports, private practice
  • Build a career with real independence
  • Enter a growing industry with strong demand

Industry Outlook

Is Massage Therapy a
Viable Career Option?

Yes, the industry is growing rapidly!

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), employment of massage therapists is projected to grow 15% from 2024 to 2034, much faster than the average for all occupations (3%). About 24,700 openings for massage therapists are projected each year.

Bureau of Labor Statistics, U.S. Department of Labor, Occupational Outlook Handbook, Massage Therapists

A report from Microsoft reveals that Massage Therapist ranked at number 22 of the least likely jobs to be replaced by AI.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions

Click or tap any question to reveal the answer.

?

Am I too old to start over?

No, 82% of all licensed massage therapists had other careers before massage (According to AMTA 2026 Research Report)

?

What if I'm not strong enough?

Massage is about technique and body mechanics — not brute strength. Your training program will guide you in these areas.

?

How long does it take to become a massage therapist?

Most programs take 6–12 months.

?

Do I need healthcare experience to start?

No, most programs are designed to take you from the very beginning, teaching you everything from the basic mechanics of touch to advanced therapeutic techniques. If you do have a background in academics or healthcare, you may be able to lighten your course load by transferring in eligible Anatomy and Physiology (A&P) credits from previous college-level schooling or medical programs

?

What are the requirements to attend massage school?

Typically, the requirements are proof of education (GED, high school diploma, college transcripts, or college diploma) and a government issued ID (driver's license, state ID, or passport). Regulations may vary by state so always check with your local board.

Your Journey

The Path to Becoming
a Massage Therapist

A straightforward roadmap from exploration to your first day on the job.

1

Explore the Career

Research the profession, understand the opportunities, and decide if massage therapy aligns with your goals and interests.

2

Request Information

Reach out to training programs, ask questions, and gather the details you need to make an informed decision.

3

Choose a Training Program

Tour schools being sure to discuss financial investments and time commitments. Select a program that fits your schedule, budget, and career goals.

4

Complete Your Training

Study topics like anatomy and physiology, business, ethics, and laws while honing your hands-on skills. Most programs can be completed in 6–12 months.

5

Prepare for Licensure

Study for and pass your licensing exam. Requirements vary by state, and your school will help guide you through the license application process.

6

Begin Your New Career

Start practicing as a licensed massage therapist. Whether you join a clinic, spa, or start your own practice — your new career begins now.
