Why Massage Therapy?

Massage therapy isn't just a job — it's a profession built around helping people feel better every single day.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions

Click or tap any question to reveal the answer.

?

How long does it take to become a massage therapist?

Most programs take between 6 and 12 months to complete, depending on the program structure and state requirements. Some accelerated programs can be completed even faster.

?

What environments can massage therapists work in?

Massage therapists work in spas, wellness centers, hospitals, sports clinics, chiropractic offices, corporate wellness programs, and in private practice. The variety of settings is one of the most appealing aspects of this career.

?

Is massage therapy a flexible career?

Absolutely. Many massage therapists set their own schedules, choose part-time or full-time work, and have the freedom to build a career around their personal life — not the other way around.

?

Do I need healthcare experience to start?

No prior healthcare experience is required. Massage therapy programs are designed to teach you everything from anatomy and physiology to hands-on technique — from the ground up.

?

Is massage therapy a good career change option?

Many massage therapists come from completely different fields — office work, retail, food service, and more. The training timeline is short, the career outlook is strong, and the work is deeply rewarding.

?

What kind of schedule do massage therapists have?

Schedules vary widely. Some therapists work traditional business hours at clinics or spas. Others build evening and weekend availability for private clients. You have significant control over when and how much you work.

Your Journey

The Path to Becoming
a Massage Therapist

A straightforward roadmap from exploration to your first day on the job.

1

Explore the Career

Research the profession, understand the opportunities, and decide if massage therapy aligns with your goals and interests.

2

Request Information

Reach out to training programs, ask questions, and gather the details you need to make an informed decision.

3

Choose a Training Program

Select a program that fits your schedule, budget, and career goals. Look for accredited programs with strong job placement support.

4

Complete Your Training

Gain hands-on skills in anatomy, physiology, and massage technique. Most programs can be completed in 6 to 12 months.

5

Prepare for Licensure

Study for and pass your licensing exam. Requirements vary by state, and your program will help guide you through the process.

6

Begin Your New Career

Start practicing as a licensed massage therapist. Whether you join a clinic, spa, or start your own practice — your new career begins now.
